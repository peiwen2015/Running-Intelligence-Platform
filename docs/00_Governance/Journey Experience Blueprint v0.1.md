# Journey Experience Blueprint

## Purpose

Design how time becomes part of the product experience.

The platform should not merely organize activities.

It should organize the runner's understanding of change over time.

## Position

Journey Product Vision answers:

`Why does the product exist?`

Journey Experience Blueprint answers:

`How does the product accompany a runner through time?`

This is not only a UX document.

It is not only a product document.

It is a time experience architecture.

## Why Time Matters

Traditional running products organize:

- activities
- workouts
- metrics
- reports

This platform organizes:

- growth
- adaptation
- coaching continuity
- long-term identity

Time is not just a dimension.

Time is the primary user experience.

## Principle 1: Time Architecture

The platform is organized by time scales.

```text
Runner Lifetime
        │
   Annual Review
        │
  Season Review
        │
Monthly Coaching
        │
 Weekly Review
        │
      Today
```

Each layer answers a different question.

## Principle 2: Questions Change with Time

`Today`

What should I do today?

`Week`

Did I execute the plan?

`Month`

Am I moving toward the goal?

`Season`

What phase of training am I entering?

`Year`

How have I changed?

`Lifetime`

Who am I becoming?

## Principle 3: Growth Over Calendar

Time should never be presented as dates alone.

Instead:

`Calendar -> Growth`

For example:

Not:

- March
- April
- May

But:

- Beginning
- Adaptation
- Build
- Peak
- Recovery

The platform should always describe change.

Not merely chronology.

## Principle 4: Continuous Coaching

The coaching relationship is continuous.

```text
Observation
    ↓
Recommendation
    ↓
Execution
    ↓
Reflection
    ↓
New Recommendation
```

Every recommendation belongs to a longer conversation.

Never an isolated report.

## Principle 5: Narrative Chapters

All product surfaces are chapters of one journey.

```text
Journey
├── Today
├── Weekly
├── Monthly
├── Shoes
├── Training
└── Metadata
```

No page should feel isolated.

Every page contributes to the same story.

A chapter should be remembered by its meaning, not by its metrics.

## Principle 6: Memory Creates Trust

The platform remembers:

- previous advice
- turning points
- milestones
- recurring patterns

Memory is not an AI feature.

Memory is the foundation of coaching.

## Principle 7: Identity Emerges Slowly

The most important output is not performance.

It is identity.

Over time the platform should help the runner answer:

`What kind of runner am I becoming?`

## Relationship to Journey Product Vision

Journey Product Vision defines:

`Why Journey exists.`

Journey Experience Blueprint defines:

`How Journey is experienced.`

## Relationship to Product Design Principles

Product Design Principles define:

`How each page guides decisions.`

Journey Experience Blueprint defines:

`How all pages together create one continuous relationship.`

## Design Implication

Every new feature should answer:

Which time scale does it belong to?

- Today?
- Week?
- Month?
- Season?
- Year?
- Lifetime?

If the answer is unclear,

the feature probably does not yet belong.

## North Star

Time becomes meaningful only when it tells a story of growth.

中文：

時間只有在述說成長時，才真正具有意義。

## Closing

Products organize information.

Great products organize time.

中文：

一般產品整理資訊；真正好的產品，整理的是時間。
