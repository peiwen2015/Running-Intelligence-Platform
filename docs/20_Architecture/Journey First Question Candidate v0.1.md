# Journey First Question Candidate v0.1

## Purpose

This is not a Journey layout draft.

This is not a timeline design.

It only records the current product finding:

**the leading candidate for Journey's first question.**

## Context

Journey is the easiest page to romanticize too early.

It can quickly collapse into:

- a visual timeline
- a chapter gallery
- a retrospective scrapbook
- a page that feels meaningful without answering a real need

That is exactly why Journey should not begin with sections, chapters, or a story format.

It should begin with the first question the runner truly wants answered when looking back across a longer stretch of training.

## What Makes Journey Different

Activity responds to an event.

Weekly responds to a short learning window.

Monthly responds to position.

Journey must respond to something else.

It is not just "more history."

It is where the platform has to answer:

**what is changing in me across time?**

That makes Journey less about session detail or month-by-month interpretation, and more about identity becoming visible.

## Candidate Review

Three candidates are worth keeping for the first reduction.

### Q1

`我正在成為什麼樣的跑者？`

Strength:

- identity-centered
- clearly different from Weekly and Monthly
- naturally long-range

Risk:

- can become too abstract if not grounded in real training evidence

### Q2

`這一段訓練，真正把我帶向了哪裡？`

Strength:

- keeps movement and direction in view
- less abstract than identity
- can connect naturally to chapters

Risk:

- still sounds somewhat editorial
- may drift closer to Monthly if not handled carefully

### Q3

`回頭看，這一章真正留下了什麼？`

Strength:

- reflective
- emotionally rich
- fits the chapter metaphor well

Risk:

- assumes Journey is already a chapter-based product
- may describe the output form before the core question is earned

## Current Read

Q1 is the leading candidate.

Not because it is the most poetic.

But because it gives Journey the clearest independent role.

If Activity asks:

`這堂課，我真正練到了什麼？`

and Weekly asks:

`這週，我到底練到了什麼？`

and Monthly asks:

`我現在在哪？`

then Journey can ask:

`我正在成為什麼樣的跑者？`

This is the first question among the five major views that is fully identity-centered.

## What Q1 Changes

If Journey starts from Q1, the page is no longer centered on:

- month summaries
- calendar recall
- chapter naming
- a prettier historical archive

It becomes centered on:

- the kind of runner that is gradually being formed
- what habits have become stable
- what kind of choices now come more naturally
- what previously difficult things have quietly become normal

That means Journey becomes different from Monthly.

Monthly answers:

`我現在在哪？`

Journey would answer:

`我正在變成誰？`

## Relationship to Other Pages

If this holds, the five major views become a coherent expansion of context:

| Page | First question | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Overview | 今天，我最該關心的是什麼？ | Attention |
| Activity | 這堂課，我真正練到了什麼？ | Single-session learning |
| Weekly | 這週，我到底練到了什麼？ | Weekly learning |
| Monthly | 我現在在哪？ | Position |
| Journey | 我正在成為什麼樣的跑者？ | Identity |

Journey does not need to compete with any of the earlier views.

It exists to answer the question none of them can answer by themselves.

## Why Q2 Still Matters

Q2 should not be discarded yet.

It may still matter inside Journey, but not necessarily as the opening question.

Possible role:

- Q1 defines who the runner is becoming
- Q2 explains where this stretch of training is taking them

In that sense, Q2 may survive later as a second-layer orientation statement.

## Validation

Q1 should remain in the lead only if it can naturally hold across at least:

1. a clear growth chapter
2. a recovery-heavy chapter
3. a disrupted or inconsistent chapter
4. a chapter where progress is subtle rather than dramatic

If it only works when the story is obviously impressive, then Journey has not yet found its real first question.

## What Failure Would Look Like

Q1 has not earned its place if Journey repeatedly drifts into:

- summarizing months
- naming phases without saying what changed in the runner
- sounding meaningful without creating recognition

Weak Journey readings would sound like:

- `這一章跑量提高了。`
- `這個月進入建構期。`
- `這是一段穩定的累積。`

These may be true.

But they do not yet answer:

`我正在成為什麼樣的跑者？`

## Next Step

Do not design Journey yet.

Do not assume chapters yet.

Use Q1 on a few real stretches of training first.

If the sentence makes the runner feel:

`對，這真的不是在說我做了什麼，而是在說我變成了什麼樣的人。`

then Journey may have found its opening.
