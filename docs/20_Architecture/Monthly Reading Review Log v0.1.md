# Monthly Reading Review Log v0.1

## Purpose

This is not a design spec.

This is not user testing.

This is a **first-reader editorial review log**.

It records what a fixed, repeatable reader is likely to feel after reading Monthly across several different months, using the current Monthly surface as it exists today.

The goal is simple:

- find where reading flows naturally
- find where reading repeatedly stalls
- find what actually stays in memory after the page is closed
- find whether the page strengthens or weakens trust in the coach voice

## Method

The role here is not "real end user."

The role is **First Reader**.

That means:

- the same reader
- the same reading questions
- different months
- comparable observations

This pass was done by reading five months in sequence:

- 2026-07
- 2026-06
- 2026-05
- 2026-04
- 2026-03

Each month was reviewed with the same five questions:

1. Where is the first stop point?
2. What section becomes easiest to skip?
3. What sentence or idea remains after closing the page?
4. After reading this letter, do I trust the coach more, less, or the same?
5. If I could remove one section, what would I cut first?

This is an editorial review method, not a UI review method.

## Reading Pass

### 2026-07

**First stop point**

The opening lands clearly.

`2026-07 目前還在進行中，但整體方向沒有偏掉。`

The first real pause happens in the transition from Opening to Timeline, because the emotional rhythm is clear, but the page quickly shifts into explanation mode.

**Easy-to-skip section**

The KPI and progress area begins to feel heavier than the letter itself.

Because this is a partial month, the page already explained that the month is still in progress. The later progress bars and signal cards are useful, but they no longer feel like the main reading path.

**What remains after reading**

This is not a month to chase.

It is a month to keep steady.

**Coach trust**

High.

The page feels calm and responsible about incomplete progress.

**What I would cut first**

I would cut the lower progress-and-signal density before I cut any part of the main letter flow.

Moderate.

## Repeating Friction

After reading the five months in sequence, the same friction points repeat:

### 1. Opening is usually strong

This is the most successful part of the page.

The opening usually leaves a clean first impression and quickly answers:

How did this month go?

### 2. Why is where reading starts to tighten

The `Why` section often shifts from coach voice into explanation voice very quickly.

This does not break the page, but it is the first place where reading becomes more analytical than conversational.

### 3. Evidence arrives after the judgment is already settled

This means evidence is often correct, but easier to skim.

That is not automatically a problem.

It only becomes a problem if evidence is taking too much space relative to what it adds.

### 4. Build months are readable, but not distinct enough from one another

2026-03, 2026-04, and 2026-05 all read coherently.

But they do not yet leave sharply different memories.

This suggests the page already has a pattern, but still needs stronger month-specific editorial contrast.

### 5. Internal labels can create small reading hesitation

Examples like:

- verdict = `負荷建構`
- phase = `基礎累積`

can both be reasonable, but they create a moment where the reader has to reconcile two frames.

That is a reading issue, not necessarily a logic issue.

## Additional Review Rounds

The first pass focused on:

- first stop point
- easiest skip
- what remained after reading

Additional passes were then used to test whether the same friction remained visible when the reader focused on different things.

### Round 2 - Distinctiveness Review

This pass asked:

**If I read several months back to back, does each month still feel like its own letter?**

Result:

- `2026-06` remains distinct
- `2026-07` remains distinct because it is partial and calmer
- `2026-03`, `2026-04`, and `2026-05` still compress together too easily in memory

The strongest repeated pattern is not confusion.

It is compression.

Three build months can all be read correctly, while still leaving too little separation between them.

### Round 3 - Trust Review

This pass asked:

**Does the page continue to sound like one coach I would believe?**

Result:

- trust remains `High` for `2026-06` and `2026-07`
- trust remains mostly intact for the build months
- however, sameness weakens authority over time more than incorrectness does

This matters because the page does not lose trust by sounding wrong.

It loses some trust by sounding too interchangeable.

### Round 4 - Subtraction Review

This pass asked:

**If one section disappeared, would the letter become weaker in meaning or only lighter in weight?**

Result:

The sections most frequently safe to reduce first are:

- lower progress density
- signal-card density
- parts of `月度趨勢`

The sections that should be protected are:

- Opening
- coach timeline
- Forward

This strengthens an earlier conclusion:

Monthly already has a working editorial spine.

Its main risk is not missing structure.

Its main risk is carrying more support than the letter actually needs.

### 6. Trust stays intact, but distinctiveness affects trust

None of the months felt untrustworthy.

But some months felt more authored than others.

That matters because Monthly is not just trying to be correct.

It is trying to feel like a letter from a coach worth listening to.

## What Works Best Right Now

The strongest current behaviors of Monthly are:

- opening with judgment instead of metrics
- using continuity from last month to next month
- letting the coach voice lead before evidence appears
- making the month feel guided, not merely summarized

The strongest protected editorial spine appears to be:

```text
Opening
   ↓
Timeline / continuity
   ↓
Why
   ↓
Forward
```

Evidence helps the page, but does not appear to be what makes the letter memorable.

## PM Summary

If this log were handed to a PM, the summary would be:

1. Monthly already works as a readable coach letter.
2. The main issue is no longer structure failure.
3. The first meaningful friction is in editorial distinctiveness, especially across similar build months.
4. The second friction is weight: evidence sections are useful, but often less necessary than their screen space suggests.
5. Trust is generally strong, but sameness weakens emotional authority across adjacent build months.
6. The next improvement should come from living with the page and tracking repeated stop points, not from redesigning the whole structure.

## Recommendation

Do not redesign Monthly yet.

Instead, keep this reading log style for repeated use:

- first stop point
- easiest skip
- one thing remembered after reading
- coach trust after reading
- one section I would cut first

If the same friction repeats across real daily reading, that friction has earned the right to be changed.
